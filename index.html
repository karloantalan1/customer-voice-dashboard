Key Takeaways
The "Teams Gap" = ~$350K/mo GPV

Add-ons + MSMP + commissions + check-in + audit trails cluster together. Teams 10+ conclude "great for solos, not for us." Every $100K+/mo GPV loss cites 2+ of these. Solving add-ons alone unblocks ~30% of lost deals.

Payments Trust = Activation Gate

Card reader failures + payout delays = ~$270K/mo GPV at risk. Combs actively targeting GG members. A trialer's first swipe and first payout are the two make-or-break moments. Fix reliability before adding features.

Gift Cards = Revenue Moat

Customers pay 2x more for platforms with GC transfers + printing. Both migration blocker and revenue driver. The "3 strikes" migration pattern (GCs + history + forms) is the #1 switch blocker.

4 Quick Wins = Immediate Credibility

Year in dates (XS), legal dropdown labels (XS), trial plan banner (S), package sale notifications (XS). Ship all 4 in one sprint to show responsiveness. Zero risk, high signal value to CX team.

Methodology & Refresh Plan

How This Was Built

127 feedback signals extracted from 5 customer-facing Slack channels (#voiceofthecustomer, #customer-experience, #research, #cx-voice-of-customer, #customer-analytics) spanning May 2024 through April 17, 2026. Each signal was coded for frequency, churn correlation, and revenue impact using the composite scoring formula:

Priority = Frequency(1-5) × Churn Signal(1-5) × Revenue Impact(1-5)

Revenue estimates are based on GPV figures explicitly mentioned in Slack posts and Dixa VOC summaries. Where no GPV was stated, estimates use segment median GPV ($8K/mo solo, $45K/mo small team, $120K/mo enterprise).

Known Limitations

Slack-only scope. This excludes Dixa raw tickets (~15K/mo), NPS verbatims, Typeform feature requests, Facebook community (~50K members), App Store reviews, and Gong call transcripts. Slack over-indexes on AE/CX-reported feedback, which skews toward larger accounts and louder voices.

Selection bias. What gets posted to Slack ≠ what matters most. High-frequency small-account issues may be underrepresented. Cross-reference with Dixa ticket volume data (Hex dashboard) before making roadmap decisions.

Revenue estimates are directional, not precise. Use for relative prioritization, not financial modeling.

Refresh Cadence

Bi-weekly refresh recommended (every other Monday). Each refresh: re-scan channels for new signals, update frequency counts, re-score items where evidence changed, flag newly trending items.

Quarterly deep dive: Cross-reference with Dixa ticket data, NPS results, and competitive intel to validate or challenge Slack-based rankings.
